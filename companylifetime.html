Timeline of requirements in the lifetime of an LTD or LLP.

LTD Company

  1. Use a formation agent, or the Companies House website to register a new company.
  2. New companies no longer have to inform HMRC that they have been formed.
  3. Register to use HMRC's website for the company for submitting tax returns etc.
  4. Issue certificates of shares to each share holder.
  5. Create a register of members and store it at the Registered Office. If you can't do this or want to store documents elsewhere, file a form with Companies House informing them.
  6. Hold your first Board of Directors meeting.
  7. Within a couple weeks return your CT41G to HMRC regarding when your company might start trading.
  8. Record all transactions (credits and debits).
  9. Write to Companies House if your Directors change, or their details change or if your Registered Office changes.
  10. File your first Annual Return on the anniversary of incorporation.
  11. File your company's Tax Return with HMRC within 9 months of the end of your first tax year. (E.g. if you incorporate in September 2012, then your tax return will be due in April 2013 and need to be filed on paper by October 2013 or online by the end of January 2014.
  12. Submit your Annual Accounts to Companies House 21 months after your company was incorporated. Subsequent Annual Accounts are due every year on this date.

Limited Liability Partnership

  1. Register each of the members as Self-Employed with HMRC.
  2. Register the LLP using a formation agent or directly with Companies House.
  3. Notify HMRC that the LLP will be filing a tax return.
  4. Create an account for the LLP on the HMRC website.
  5. Create a register of members to be stored at the Registered Office. If you can't do this or want to store documents elsewhere, file a form with Companies House informing them.
  6. Record all transactions (credits and debits).
  7. Write to Companies House if your Partners change, or their details change or if your Registered Office changes.
  8. File your first Annual Return on the anniversary of incorporation.
  9. File your individual partners self-assessment tax returns on paper within 6 months or electronically using the HMRC website within 9 months of the end of the first tax year.
  10. File your LLPs first tax return on paper within 6 months or electronically using a paid software solution within 9 months of the first tax year.
  11. Submit your Annual Accounts to Companies House 21 months after your company was incorporated. Subsequent Annual Accounts are due every year on this date.
